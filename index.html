§5 · Market Alignment Strategy
Why AI Training, Not Inference
AI training clusters require 50–100 MW contiguous power blocks with consistent delivery — not millisecond latency. This site's suburban grid capacity and large contiguous pad directly serve training workloads, where GPU compute density and sustained power are the constraints that matter.
Ex-Urban Grid Advantage
Urban markets (Chicago, Columbus, N. Virginia) face grid saturation, interconnect queues of 3–5 years, and land scarcity. This Indiana corridor offers available transmission capacity, shorter interconnect timelines, and developable acreage at a fraction of urban land cost — with comparable fiber access via short extensions.
2026–2028 Power Window
Hyperscalers are contracting 2028 and 2029 power delivery windows now. This site's 24-month development path to energization aligns with that demand curve. Neocloud operators (CoreWeave, Lambda, Crusoe) are better initial tenants than hyperscalers — faster contracting cycles, more flexible on site specifications.
§6 · Analyst's Note

The technically difficult part of this work was not finding a large parcel — it was validating whether the transmission corridor could actually support 150 MW at the interconnect point, not just at the substation. That required direct conversations with utility planners at Duke Energy and NIPSCO, not just desktop research. Grid capacity that appears available on paper frequently evaporates when you account for existing queue commitments and distribution-level constraints upstream.

The insight that unlocked value on this site was recognizing that the 7.5-acre wetland pocket, which initially appeared to bisect the most attractive development area, could be resolved through buffer realignment rather than full avoidance. By adjusting the pad boundary to treat the wetland buffer as a perimeter setback rather than an interior constraint, we recovered approximately 60 acres of net developable area that a less careful analysis would have written off entirely.

The risk I mitigated early was entitlement sequencing. By engaging Tippecanoe County planning before any formal application — and confirming data center is a conditionally permitted use in the existing zoning class — I eliminated the rezoning risk that is a common deal-killer on agricultural parcels. That conversation, had early, compressed the anticipated entitlement timeline by 6–9 months and allowed the site to be positioned as substantially de-risked for wholesale disposition.
